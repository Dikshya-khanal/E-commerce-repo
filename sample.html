1. Introduction & Objective

E-commerce refers to the buying and selling of goods and services over the internet. This project aims to explore its impact and significance.

2. The History and Evolution of E-Commerce

Originally started in the 1960s, e-commerce grew with the rise of the internet in the 1990s. In Nepal, platforms like Daraz and SastoDeal have revolutionized online trade.

3. Key E-Commerce Business Models

  • B2B - Business to Business
  • B2C - Business to Consumer
  • C2C - Consumer to Consumer

4. Essential E-Commerce Technologies & Platforms

Key platforms include Shopify, WooCommerce, and Magento.

5. Strategies for Online Marketing & Sales

Techniques include SEO, social media marketing, and targeted ads.

6. Supply Chain Management & Logistics

Efficient logistics involve inventory control, warehousing, and delivery tracking.

7. Legal and Ethical Considerations in E-Commerce

Privacy laws, data security, and consumer protection policies are critical.

8. Future Trends & Predictions

AI-driven recommendations, blockchain security, and faster deliveries are key future trends.

9. Local Example: A Tea Shop Using E-Commerce

Consider a local tea shop creating an online store to sell organic teas with home delivery options.

10. Conclusion

E-commerce is rapidly transforming businesses, making trade more accessible globally.
